Power of Yeast

Yeast is a type of fungus, a tiny living organism you can't see with the naked eye. It's a single-celled organism, meaning it's made up of just one cell.

Do you know how the yeast respires?
Yeast can respire in two ways:

1. Aerobic Respiration: This happens when there's plenty of oxygen around. Yeast uses oxygen to break down sugar (like glucose) into carbon dioxide, water, and energy.
2. Anaerobic Respiration (Fermentation): When there's not enough oxygen, yeast switches to fermentation. In this process, it breaks down sugar into carbon dioxide, alcohol, and energy.
