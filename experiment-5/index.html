Apple Browning Experiment

Apple browning is a common phenomenon that occurs when the flesh of an apple is exposed to air. This process is called enzymatic browning, which is caused by a reaction between oxygen and certain enzymes in the apple, primarily polyphenol oxidase (PPO). When an apple is cut or bruised, the cell walls break, allowing oxygen to react with the PPO enzyme, leading to the production of brown-coloured compounds called melanin.

This experiment explores the factors that affect apple browning and investigates ways to slow it down. By testing different treatments such as sugar, saltwater, or vinegar, we can determine which methods are most effective in preventing browning. Understanding this process is important in food science, as it helps improve food preservation techniques and reduce waste.
